# Technical Review of Nickel Electrowinning Processes

## Overview
Nickel electrowinning is a critical process in hydrometallurgy used to produce high-purity nickel cathodes from an aqueous solution. 

## Process Parameters
The optimal catholyte circulation rate for maximizing current efficiency and minimizing dendrite growth is generally between 0.15 m/s and 0.25 m/s. 

## Equipment
Modern cells use lead-alloy anodes and stainless steel cathodes. The temperature of the electrolyte is typically maintained at 60°C to 65°C to balance conductivity and energy consumption.

## Conclusion
Maintaining a strict flow velocity range is essential for product quality.
